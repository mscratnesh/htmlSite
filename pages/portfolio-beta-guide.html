?? What is Portfolio Beta?

Portfolio Beta is a powerful metric that measures how your equity portfolio moves relative to the broader market (Nifty 50). Understanding your portfolio's beta helps you make informed decisions about risk management and hedging strategies.

??? How to Hedge Using Portfolio Beta

Once you calculate your portfolio beta, you can use Nifty Put Options to create a precise hedge that protects your portfolio value during market downturns while maintaining upside potential.

Use our Portfolio Beta Calculator to determine how your holdings correlate with Nifty 50. Simply input your stock symbols and investment amounts.

2

Calculate Beta-Adjusted Exposure

Adjusted Exposure = Portfolio Value × Portfolio Beta

3

Determine Required Nifty Lots

Lots Needed = Adjusted Exposure ÷ (Nifty Spot × 50)

4

Execute Your Hedge

Buy the calculated number of Nifty Put options at ATM or slightly OTM strike prices. Choose 1-3 month expiry for cost-effectiveness.

? Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How often should I recalculate my portfolio beta?

A: Recalculate whenever you make significant portfolio changes (adding/removing stocks) or at least monthly. Beta can change over time as company fundamentals and market conditions evolve.

Q: What if my portfolio beta is less than 1?

A: A beta less than 1 means your portfolio is less volatile than the market (defensive). You'll need fewer Nifty lots to hedge, making protection cheaper. This is common for portfolios heavy in defensive sectors like IT, Pharma, or FMCG.

Q: Should I hedge 100% of my portfolio?

A: Not necessarily. Many investors hedge 50-70% of their portfolio to balance protection costs with risk management. Full hedging (100%) is expensive and typically used only during extreme uncertainty.

Q: What's the typical cost of hedging?

A: Option premiums typically cost 1-3% of portfolio value per month, depending on market volatility (VIX). During high volatility, costs can be higher but protection is more valuable.

Q: Can I use this for mid-cap or small-cap stocks?

A: Yes, but effectiveness varies. Mid-caps and small-caps may have lower correlation with Nifty 50. For small-cap heavy portfolios, consider using Nifty Smallcap 50 or Nifty Midcap 50 futures/options if available.

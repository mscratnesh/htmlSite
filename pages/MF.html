Mutual Funds

📊 Understanding Mutual Funds in India

Mutual funds in India are professionally managed investment vehicles that pool money from investors to buy a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other assets. They are regulated by SEBI and categorized mainly into equity, debt, hybrid, solution-oriented, and other types, as outlined in our prior discussion on 2026 classifications.

⚙ How Mutual Funds Work

Units are allotted based on Net Asset Value (NAV), calculated daily as total assets minus liabilities divided by units outstanding. Investors buy/sell via lump sum or Systematic Investment Plans (SIPs), with returns from dividends, capital gains, or interest.

Returns vary by risk: equity funds suit long-term growth (5+ years), debt for stability/short-term, hybrids for balance. Expense ratios (1-2%) and exit loads apply; taxation differs—equity LTCG over ₹1.25 lakh at 12.5% (post-1 year), debt per slab.

📌 Key Investment Factors

  • Risk-Return Profile: Equity (high risk/high return), Debt (low risk/low return).
  • AMC Role: Fund managers actively/passively track benchmarks like Nifty 50.
  • SIP Benefits: Rupee-cost averaging mitigates volatility; ideal for salaried investors like you tracking Nifty/options.

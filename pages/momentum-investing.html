Momentum Investing

📈 Overview

Momentum investing flips the "buy low, sell high" mantra by buying high and selling higher, capitalizing on persisting upward price trends in outperforming assets. Academic research confirms it often beats benchmarks like Nifty 500, as winners continue rallying due to investor herding and fundamentals.

📊 Strategy Overview: Sharpe Ratio Momentum in Nifty 500

Filter Nifty 500 stocks by Sharpe Ratio (risk-adjusted returns: (Return - Risk-Free Rate)/Volatility), ranking all ~500 via a spreadsheet. Select top 30; invest equally in top 10 for diversified exposure. Rebalance monthly (or weekly) on the 1st: Sell any holdings outside top 30, divide proceeds equally into new top-10 entries. This systematic rotation captures momentum while limiting drawdowns.

📝 Implementation Rules

  • Universe: Nifty 500 (large/mid-caps for liquidity in delivery trades).
  • Initial Allocation: Equal weight top 10 (e.g., ₹10L portfolio → ₹1L each).
  • Rebalancing: Monthly check—if 3 stocks drop out, sell them (get ~₹3L), buy 3 new top-10 equally (₹1L each).
  • Lumpsum Timing: Deploy major cash during rebalances or post-10-15% quarterly corrections.
  • Cash Buffer: Park 5% in safe ETFs like Nippon India ETF Nifty Bees or gold SGBs; deploy on dips for opportunistic buys.

Stock Key Ratios

Fundamental analysis evaluates a stock's intrinsic value by studying a company's financial health, operations, and external factors, contrasting with technical analysis that focuses on price charts. It helps long-term investors like you identify undervalued opportunities in Indian markets (e.g., Nifty stocks) for portfolio building alongside options trading.

Key Variables

These core metrics, derived from financial statements (balance sheet, P&L, cash flow), guide valuation.

Variable Description Formula/Example
Earnings Per Share (EPS) Profit per share; shows profitability Net Income / Shares Outstanding
Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Valuation relative to earnings; lower may indicate undervaluation Price / EPS (e.g., Nifty avg ~22x)
Price-to-Book (P/B) Compares market value to book value; useful for banks Market Price / Book Value per Share
Debt-to-Equity (D/E) Leverage measure; <1 preferred for stability Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity
Return on Equity (ROE) Efficiency in generating profits from equity Net Income / Shareholders' Equity (e.g., >15% strong)
Dividend Yield Income from dividends; suits conservative strategies Annual Dividend / Price
Revenue Growth Top-line expansion; quarterly YoY check (Current - Prior Revenue) / Prior
Free Cash Flow (FCF) Cash after capex; funds dividends/growth Operating Cash Flow - Capex
PEG Ratio P/E adjusted for growth; <1 undervalued P/E / EPS Growth Rate
Current Ratio Liquidity; >1.5 ideal Current Assets / Current Liabilities
